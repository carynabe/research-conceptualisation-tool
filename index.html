Masters research tools

Three tools to help you develop the thinking behind your research proposal. Each tool builds on the previous one. Work through them in order.

On thinking order versus writing order: Your proposal is read in the order: purpose → problem → questions → literature → design. But it is developed in the reverse order — you discover your PPSQ through working with the literature, and you justify your design through working with your questions. These tools follow the thinking order. The writing order comes after.

Tool 2 — Before your presentation
Test drive your research — builder
Tests the logical alignment of your research design — from PPSQ through conceptual framework, methodology, methods, data, and ethics. Produces an alignment map that shows where your chain holds and where it needs strengthening before you build your slides.
Conceptual framework Methodology Research design Alignment

Tool 3 — For peer reviewers
Test drive your research — peer reviewer
Develops your capacity to evaluate research design logic — the same skill a proposal panel uses. You reason through each criterion before scoring, producing a specific and justified review. Reviewing someone else's design prepares you to defend your own.
Peer review Evaluation Panel preparation Logical coherence

These tools develop thinking, not writing. Your responses are held in your browser session only and are not submitted or stored anywhere. Use the export function in each tool to save your work before closing. Each tool takes approximately 30–45 minutes to complete thoughtfully.
