Test drive your research — peer reviewer

You are learning to evaluate research design logic. This is the same skill a proposal panel uses. Your feedback should be reasoned, specific, and useful to the presenter.

What you are doing here: A research proposal panel does not just score a study — it evaluates whether the logic holds. You are practising that same evaluative move. Before you score any criterion, you are asked to reason through it. The score follows the reasoning, not the other way around. Your review is anonymous. It counts toward your grade — and it prepares you for your own panel.
What you are evaluating: Structural and conceptual alignment — whether the researcher has intentionally negotiated the flow from PPSQ through conceptual framework to research design, and whether that flow is logically coherent and justified at each step.
Brief methodological orientation — before you review
Qualitative

Concerned with meaning and interpretation. Valid when the research questions ask how or why something occurs. Look for: consistency between interpretive claims and the data sources used.

Document analysis

Uses existing texts or records as data. Valid when the phenomenon is embedded in documented evidence. Look for: clear criteria for document selection and a defined analytical approach.

Mixed methods

Combines qualitative and quantitative approaches. Valid only when neither approach alone can answer the research questions. Look for: explicit justification for combining, not just using both.
